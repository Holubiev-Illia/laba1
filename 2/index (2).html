The Starry Night - Vincent van Gogh

One of the most recognized paintings in Western culture, created in June 1889. The night sky filled with swirling clouds, stars, and a crescent moon over a sleeping village with a prominent church spire.

Exhibition Highlight: Masters of Light

Experience the revolutionary techniques of the Dutch Golden Age masters, featuring works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Frans Hals. This collection showcases their masterful use of light and shadow, bringing scenes to life with unprecedented realism and emotional depth.

The exhibition explores how these painters revolutionized the use of chiaroscuro and created intimate portraits and scenes that continue to captivate viewers centuries later.

The Night Watch - Rembrandt

Rembrandt's largest and most famous painting, remarkable for its colossal size, the dramatic use of light and shadow, and the perception of motion in what would traditionally have been a static military group portrait.
